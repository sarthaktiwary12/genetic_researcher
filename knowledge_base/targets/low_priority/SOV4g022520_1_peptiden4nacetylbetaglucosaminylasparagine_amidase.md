**Known/Predicted Function:**
*   **Enzymatic Activity:** PNGase (Peptide-N4-(N-acetyl-beta-glucosaminyl)asparagine amidase) is an enzyme that cleaves the N-glycan chain from N-glycosylated proteins at the asparagine residue. This reaction releases a free N-glycan and a deglycosylated protein, converting the asparagine to an aspartic acid residue.
*   **Protein Quality Control (ERAD):** In eukaryotes, PNGase is a key component of the ER-associated degradation (ERAD) pathway. Misfolded or terminally misprocessed N-glycosylated proteins are retrotranslocated from the endoplasmic reticulum (ER) to the cytosol. In the cytosol, PNGase deglycosylates these proteins, which then become targets for ubiquitination and subsequent degradation by the 26S proteasome. This process is crucial for maintaining cellular proteostasis and preventing the accumulation of toxic protein aggregates.
*   **Plant Homologs:** *Arabidopsis thaliana* possesses several PNGase homologs (e.g., AtPNGase/At2g39180, At5g07400, At3g05210). Studies in *Arabidopsis* confirm their cytosolic localization and involvement in protein quality control, particularly under stress conditions (e.g., heat, drought, salt stress), where efficient turnover of damaged proteins is critical.
*   **Other Roles:** While primarily associated with ERAD, PNGase activity might also be involved in the processing of specific glycoproteins for vacuolar degradation or in modulating the activity/stability of N-glycosylated signaling molecules by removing their glycan moieties.
