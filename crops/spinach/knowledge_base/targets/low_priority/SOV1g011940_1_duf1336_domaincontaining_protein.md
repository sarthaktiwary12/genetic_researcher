        *   **Cell wall modification**: Remodeling of cell wall polysaccharides.
        *   **Signal transduction**: Production or degradation of specific sugar signals.
    *   **Alpha-xylosidases** are involved in the degradation of xylans, major components of plant cell walls.
*   **INFERRED (Stress/Defense Context)**: Some plant DUF1336 proteins, including those with predicted GH31 activity, have been implicated in various stress responses (e.g., drought, salinity, cold) and plant-microbe interactions. They can be induced by pathogens or abiotic stress.

### 2. GERMINATION RELEVANCE

**INFERRED (Based on GH31 activity)**:
If SOV1g011940.1 functions as a glycosyl hydrolase (GH31), its relevance to seed germination and early seedling development could be multifaceted:
*   **Storage Reserve Mobilization**: Alpha-glucosidases are essential for breaking down stored starch and oligosaccharides in the endosperm/cotyledons into usable sugars (glucose) to fuel embryo growth. This process is tightly regulated by hormones like GA and ABA.
*   **Cell Wall Remodeling**: During radicle protrusion, the cell walls of the embryo and surrounding tissues (e.g., endosperm, testa) must undergo controlled loosening and modification. Glycosyl hydrolases are key enzymes in this process, facilitating cell expansion and breaking dormancy.
*   **Signal Transduction**: Specific oligosaccharides can act as signaling molecules. A GH31 enzyme could either produce such signals (e.g., elicitors) or degrade them, thereby influencing developmental processes or stress responses during germination.
*   **Stress/Defense Responses**: Germination is a vulnerable stage. If SOV1g011940.1 is part of a stress or defense pathway, its activity could be induced by environmental cues or microbial presence. Activating defense pathways during germination can divert resources from growth, potentially delaying or reducing germination success.
