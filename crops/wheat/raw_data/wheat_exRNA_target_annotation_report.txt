SECTION 7: NOTES ON METHODOLOGY AND LIMITATIONS
================================================================================

1. Gene models are from IWGSC RefSeq v1.1 annotation. Some IDs may not be
   present in newer v2.1 annotation. Gene suffixes like ".1" denote specific
   transcripts.

2. Annotations were retrieved from EnsemblPlants (Release 62), UniProtKB/
   TrEMBL, InterPro, and PANTHER databases. Many wheat proteins are
   unreviewed (TrEMBL) and rely on computational prediction.

3. Homeolog identification in hexaploid wheat is complicated by:
   - Chromosomal rearrangements between subgenomes
   - Gene duplications independent of polyploidy
   - Large-scale translocations (notably 4A/5A/7B)
   - NLR and F-box gene families undergoing birth-and-death evolution

4. The unusually high number of F-box proteins (6/75 = 8%) and receptor-
   like kinases (5/75 = 6.7%) in this target list suggests these gene
   families may be preferentially targeted by bacterial extracellular
   sRNAs. Both families are involved in immune signaling:
   - F-box proteins degrade immune suppressors via proteasome
   - LRR-RLKs are the first line of PAMP perception

5. The presence of NB-LRR resistance genes (#15, #75), defense ABC
   transporters (#31), and antifungal proteins (#26) further supports
   a model where bacterial sRNAs target plant immune components.
