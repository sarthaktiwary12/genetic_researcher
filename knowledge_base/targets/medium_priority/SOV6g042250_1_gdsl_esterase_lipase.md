### 1. FUNCTION: GDSL Esterase/Lipase

**KNOWN FACTS:**
*   GDSL esterase/lipases (GELPs) constitute a large and diverse family of serine hydrolases found across prokaryotes and eukaryotes, including plants. They are characterized by a conserved GDSL motif near the N-terminus.
*   Their enzymatic activity involves the hydrolysis of various ester bonds, including thioesters, phospholipids, galactolipids, and waxes.
*   In plants, GELPs are implicated in a wide array of physiological processes, including:
    *   **Lipid metabolism**: breakdown of storage lipids, cuticular wax biosynthesis, membrane lipid remodeling.
    *   **Cell wall remodeling**: modification of pectin, hemicellulose, or other cell wall components.
    *   **Development**: pollen development, anther dehiscence, seed development, root hair formation.
    *   **Stress responses**: defense against pathogens and herbivores, abiotic stress tolerance (drought, salinity, cold).
    *   **Hormone signaling**: involvement in jasmonate (JA) biosynthesis and signaling, potentially interacting with other hormone pathways.
