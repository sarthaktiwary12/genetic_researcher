### 1. FUNCTION: Known/Predicted Function of SOV1g048290.1

**KNOWN FACTS (based on Arabidopsis homologs):**
*   **Glutamate Receptors (GLRs)** in plants are a family of ligand-gated ion channels, structurally and functionally analogous to mammalian ionotropic glutamate receptors.
*   They are primarily known to mediate **calcium influx (Ca2+)** into the cytosol upon activation by various ligands, including glutamate and potentially other amino acids or even mechanical stimuli.
*   Plant GLRs are involved in a wide array of physiological processes:
    *   **Nutrient sensing**: Sensing amino acids (e.g., glutamate) as nutrient signals.
    *   **Stress responses**: Involved in responses to various abiotic stresses (drought, salinity, cold, heat, wounding) and biotic stresses (pathogen defense). They often act as early sensors of stress.
    *   **Development**: Regulating root architecture, pollen tube growth, stomatal movement, and potentially overall growth.
    *   **Long-distance signaling**: Proposed roles in systemic acquired resistance and systemic wound signaling.

**INFERRED CONCLUSIONS / SPECULATIVE HYPOTHESES:**
