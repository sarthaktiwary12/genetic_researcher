### 1. FUNCTION: What is the known/predicted function of this gene?

**KNOWN FACTS (from Arabidopsis/model plants):**
*   **HIRA (Histone Regulator A)** is a highly conserved protein that functions as a core component of the replication-independent histone chaperone complex.
*   Its primary role is to deposit the histone variant **H3.3** into chromatin, particularly at actively transcribed gene bodies and regulatory regions, independently of DNA replication.
*   In plants, the HIRA complex typically includes HIRA, ASF1 (Anti-Silencing Function 1), and CAF-1 (Chromatin Assembly Factor 1).
*   H3.3 deposition is generally associated with **active transcription** and maintaining an open, euchromatic chromatin state.
*   HIRA is crucial for **chromatin assembly, maintenance of genome integrity, and regulation of gene expression** in response to developmental cues and environmental stresses.
*   In *Arabidopsis thaliana*, HIRA has been implicated in various developmental processes and, importantly, in **plant immunity and defense responses**, often interacting with the salicylic acid (SA) pathway.

**UNCERTAINTY IN ANNOTATION:**
*   The annotation "Protein HIRA" is broad but generally refers to the canonical HIRA protein family. Assuming SOV6g036290.1 is a functional homolog of Arabidopsis HIRA, its general role in H3.3 deposition and chromatin regulation is highly probable. Specific functional domains would need to be confirmed for spinach.
