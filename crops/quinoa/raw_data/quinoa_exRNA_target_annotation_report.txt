SECTION 1: SPECIES AND EXPERIMENTAL CONTEXT
================================================================================

SPECIES: Chenopodium quinoa (quinoa)
- Allotetraploid (2n = 4x = 36; AABB genome)
- Parentage: C. pallidicaule (A genome) x C. suecicum (B genome)
- Family: Amaranthaceae (Caryophyllales)
- Key traits: halophyte, betalain-producing (not anthocyanin), saponin-rich seed coat
- Reference: Jarvis et al. 2017 Nature; Yasui et al. 2016 DNA Research 23:535-546
- Genome: ~1.5 Gb; 44,776 gene models (Jarvis 2017); 54,438 protein-coding genes (Yasui 2016)
- Annotation: 97.3% BUSCO completeness; genes annotated against Arabidopsis BLASTP,
  Pfam domains, and RNAseq from multiple tissues

EXPERIMENTAL CONTEXT:
These 31 transcripts were predicted as targets of bacterial extracellular small RNAs (sRNAs)
that downregulate quinoa gene expression during seed germination. Cross-kingdom sRNA
communication (plant-bacteria) has been documented in multiple systems (Trends in Genetics
2025; PMID: related cross-kingdom RNAi literature). Quinoa seeds are 100% inhabited by
diverse Bacillus spp. endophytes with vertical transmission (Pankievicz et al. 2016, PMC4722091),
making bacterial sRNA-mediated regulation during germination biologically plausible.

Scaffold location: C_Quinoa_Scaffold_1995: 3,507,903-3,511,700 (reverse strand)

Protein family / domain architecture:
The EnsemblPlants annotation classifies AUR62012347 as a "Nontranslating CDS," meaning
the annotated ORF is present but the current genome model does not produce a protein
product. This is significant because AUR62012346-RA (one locus away on the same scaffold)
encodes the functional CqCYP76AD1, the quinoa ortholog of beet CYP76AD1 involved in
betalain biosynthesis. AUR62012347 is almost certainly a tandem duplicate of CqCYP76AD1.
Recent work (Ge et al. 2025, Global investigation CqCYP76AD and CqDODA, ScienceDirect;
Nagatoshi et al. 2023, PMC10453985; Cai et al. 2025, Stress Biology) identified extensive
CYP76AD tandem duplication in quinoa, generating 17 CqCYP76AD copies plus numerous
pseudogene-like partial copies. The v1.0 genome annotation likely placed this tandem copy
in the nontranslating category.

ANNOTATION CORRECTION FLAG:
The original study description labels AUR62012347 as "CYP76AD1-like or DODA-like." Based
on genomic proximity to AUR62012346 (CqCYP76AD1) and the CYP76AD tandem-duplication
architecture documented in quinoa, this locus is most likely a CYP76AD-alpha clade
pseudogene or truncated copy, NOT a functional CYP76AD1 or DODA. The labeling as a
functional betalain enzyme requires experimental validation. In the Stress Biology 2025
paper (Cai et al.), a structurally similar locus (Cqu0091301) was restored to full function
only by a ~4-kb genomic insertion absent in the reference genome, illustrating how reference-
based annotations can misclassify functional variants as pseudogenes.

Arabidopsis ortholog: AT4G31500 (CYP76B6 subfamily; P450 monooxygenase superfamily)
                     or closest: AT4G25360 (CYP76AD1-like, not present in Arabidopsis
                     as Arabidopsis lacks betalains; best Arabidopsis structural homolog)
Functional category: SECONDARY METABOLISM / BETALAIN BIOSYNTHESIS
Known role in seed germination:
CqCYP76AD, CqDODA, and CqGTs genes showed significant expression variation in seeds
during germination and in response to cold stress (Nagatoshi et al. 2023, Plant Signaling
& Behavior). CYP76AD enzymes catalyze the hydroxylation of L-tyrosine to L-DOPA, the
first committed step in betalain (betacyanin + betaxanthin) biosynthesis. Betalains function
as antioxidants and potentially as osmoprotectants during seed imbibition.
Expression: Expressed in colored tissues; seed coat pigmentation locus. CYP76AD1 expression
was NOT detected in quinoa seed shotgun proteomics (Supplementary, Cai et al. 2025),
suggesting the AUR62012346/12347 locus is primarily active in vegetative coloration.
Amaranthaceae-specific feature: YES — betalain biosynthesis is an Amaranthaceae/
Caryophyllales synapomorphy; absent in Arabidopsis and all other angiosperm families
except Caryophyllales.
Confidence: MEDIUM (locus context from literature; direct protein function unconfirmed
due to nontranslating annotation)

UniProt: Not individually deposited; family data in PMC10650088
Protein size: 640 amino acids; Chromosome 2 location (Chr02: 54,650,085-54,654,440)

Protein family / domain architecture:
HAK/KUP/KT (High-Affinity K+/K+ Uptake/K+ Transporter) superfamily; APC superfamily
(amino acid-polyamine-organocation). Contains 12-14 transmembrane domains. HAK5-type
(Cluster I, Clade IB) high-affinity potassium transporter. The protein facilitates K+
influx into cells; in salt-accumulating tissues, HAK5-type transporters drive K+ import
to maintain osmotic balance against vacuolar Na+.

Arabidopsis ortholog: AT4G13420 (AtHAK5) — the primary high-affinity K+ uptake
transporter in Arabidopsis roots under K+ deficiency
Beta vulgaris ortholog: BvHAK5-type (closely related in Amaranthaceae)
Functional category: ION TRANSPORT / POTASSIUM HOMEOSTASIS / ABIOTIC STRESS
Known role in seed germination:
HAK5-type transporters are critical for K+ acquisition under low-K+ conditions. K+ is
essential for protein synthesis, enzyme activation, and osmotic adjustment during
germination. AtHAK5 is the dominant K+ uptake system in Arabidopsis roots at micromolar
K+ concentrations. In quinoa, CqHAK5 belongs to Cluster I (Clade IB), the clade
associated with high-affinity K+ influx; specific germination expression data for CqHAK5
was reported as "minimal" in most vegetative tissues in the Ren et al. 2023 study, but
K+ transporter activity is expected to be critical during seed imbibition and radicle
emergence. Under K+-deficient conditions, CqHAK5-type expression is activated via
CIPK23/CBL1/9 complex signaling.
Expression: Root-preferential expression (Cluster I HAKs); low baseline across tissues;
induced by K+ deficiency and saline/alkaline stress
Halophyte adaptation: The quinoa HAK5-type transporter family (30 members vs. ~13 in
Arabidopsis) reflects expansion consistent with halophytic adaptation and enhanced
K+/Na+ discrimination.
Confidence: HIGH (confirmed by Ren et al. 2023, PMC10650088 — Table 1 mapping)

SECTION 7: AMARANTHACEAE / CARYOPHYLLALES-SPECIFIC FEATURES
================================================================================

1. BETALAIN BIOSYNTHESIS (AUR62012347)
   Betalain pathway genes (CYP76AD, DODA, GT families) are unique to Caryophyllales —
   the order that replaced anthocyanins with nitrogen-containing betalain pigments.
   The quinoa genome contains 17 CqCYP76AD, 18 CqDODA, and 24 CqGT family members,
   far exceeding the 2-3 copies in beet, indicating extensive tandem duplication.
   The v1.0 annotation contains pseudogene-like nontranslating copies (AUR62012347)
   adjacent to functional copies (AUR62012346), illustrating ongoing birth-and-death
   evolution in this pathway.
   Citation: Nagatoshi et al. 2023 (PMC10453985); Ge et al. 2025 (ScienceDirect);
   Cai et al. 2025 (Stress Biology)

2. EXPANDED HAK FAMILY (AUR62010943 = CqHAK5)
   Quinoa has 30 CqHAK members vs. ~13 in Arabidopsis. This expansion reflects
   halophytic adaptation requiring multiple K+ transport strategies for K+/Na+
   discrimination. CqHAK5 drives K+ influx into leaf salt bladder cells for osmotic
   balance in the specialized epidermal salt-sequestering structures unique to
   Chenopodioideae and other Amaranthaceae subfamilies.
   Citation: Ren et al. 2023 (PMC10650088)

3. EXPANDED CNGC FAMILY (AUR62044372 = CqCNGC14)
   34 CqCNGC genes (vs. 20 in Arabidopsis). Expanded CNGC family in quinoa likely
   supports enhanced Ca2+ signaling capacity across diverse stress conditions
   experienced in quinoa's natural Andean habitat (salt, heat, cold, UV, altitude).
   Citation: Zhang et al. 2024/2025 (SSRN 5070311)

4. LINEAGE-SPECIFIC PROTEINS (AUR62011287, AUR62040122)
   Proteins with very few orthologs outside Amaranthaceae likely represent innovations
   associated with: saponin biosynthesis (quinoa-specific terpenoid sapogenin
   accumulation in seed coat), betalain pathway accessories, or halophytic adaptation
   mechanisms absent in most other plant families.

5. QUINOA NBS-LRR EXPANSION (AUR62021543 candidate)
   183 NBS proteins identified in quinoa (Bhatt et al. 2024, PMID:39100881), reflecting
   the allotetraploid genome doubling effect on R gene family size. Targeting of an
   NLR gene by bacterial sRNAs represents a sophisticated immune evasion strategy.

LIMITATIONS OF THIS ANALYSIS:
1. Direct BLAST searches against Arabidopsis or Beta vulgaris proteomes were not performed
   computationally (no local database access); ortholog inference relied on published
   studies and EnsemblPlants projections.
2. 26/31 targets remain without confirmed gene names due to: (a) absence of named gene
   family studies covering these specific loci, (b) EnsemblPlants summary pages not
   displaying projected function for all genes, (c) AUR62045001 absent from databases.
3. The quinoa v1.0 genome annotation contains known quality issues: some gene models
   are annotated as nontranslating when they may encode functional proteins in specific
   cultivar contexts (Cai et al. 2025 structural variation data).
4. Expression data from seed germination transcriptomics (Ren et al. 2022 BMC Plant Biol)
   did not specifically report these 31 AUR IDs in accessible format; germination
   expression data for each target therefore relies on indirect evidence.
5. The "sRNA targeting" mechanism has not been biochemically validated for any of these
   31 targets. The target list is computationally predicted.
