These enzymes catalyze the removal of SUMO from SUMOylated target proteins (desumoylation).

This post-translational modification is crucial for diverse plant processes, including development, hormone signaling, and stress responses.

For instance, *Arabidopsis thaliana* mutants lacking SUMO proteases such as OTS1 and OTS2 (OVERLY TOLERANT TO SALT1/2) exhibit enhanced ABA sensitivity, reduced germination rates under stress, and delayed seedling development. Conversely, overexpression of some SUMO proteases can lead to ABA insensitivity and improved germination. This suggests that desumoylation by SUMO proteases often *antagonizes* ABA signaling or promotes germination.

However, by influencing ABA pathways, SUMO proteases indirectly impact the crucial ABA/GA balance that dictates germination success.
    *   **Stress Responses:** SUMOylation is a rapid and reversible modification that helps plants adapt to various environmental stresses (e.g., osmotic stress, heat, cold), which are often encountered during germination. SUMO proteases are critical for fine-tuning these responses by reversing SUMOylation.
*   **INFERRED:** Based on the general role of SUMO proteases, SOV4g018960.1 would be involved in regulating the SUMOylation status of proteins that control the balance between dormancy and germination, hormone sensitivity, and stress tolerance during spinach seed germination and early seedling growth.
