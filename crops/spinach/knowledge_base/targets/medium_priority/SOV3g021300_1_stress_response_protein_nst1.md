#### 1. FUNCTION: Known/Predicted Function

*   **KNOWN (from *Arabidopsis* homologs):** The annotation "NST1" in plants typically refers to "NAC Secondary Wall Thickening Promoting Factor 1." In *Arabidopsis thaliana*, AtNST1 (and its close paralogs AtNST2 and AtNST3/SND1) are well-characterized master regulators of secondary cell wall biosynthesis. They directly activate genes involved in the synthesis of cellulose, xylan, and lignin, components crucial for mechanical strength and water transport in vascular tissues and fiber cells. NAC transcription factors are also broadly implicated in various stress responses, senescence, and developmental processes.
*   **INFERRED (for SOV3g021300.1):** Given the annotation and the "defense_immunity" pathway assignment, it is highly probable that SOV3g021300.1 is a homolog of *Arabidopsis* NST1 or a closely related NAC domain transcription factor. However, its assignment to "defense_immunity" suggests an additional or overlapping role in stress responses, possibly involving stress-induced cell wall modifications or general defense signaling.
*   **UNCERTAINTY:** While "NST1" strongly points to a NAC TF involved in secondary cell walls, the general "Stress response protein" part of the annotation is less specific. If SOV3g021300.1 is not a NAC TF homolog, its function could be entirely different. However, based on common plant gene nomenclature, the NAC TF interpretation is the most likely.
