#### 1. FUNCTION: Known/Predicted Function

**KNOWN FACTS**:
Pentatricopeptide repeat (PPR) proteins constitute a large and diverse family of RNA-binding proteins predominantly found in plants. They are characterized by tandem arrays of a degenerate 35-amino acid motif (PPR motif). The vast majority of PPR proteins are targeted to mitochondria and chloroplasts, where they play crucial roles in virtually all aspects of post-transcriptional RNA metabolism. These functions include:
*   **RNA editing**: Modifying specific nucleotides in organellar transcripts (e.g., C-to-U conversions).
*   **RNA splicing**: Facilitating the removal of introns from pre-mRNAs.
*   **RNA cleavage and processing**: Maturation of tRNAs, rRNAs, and mRNAs.
*   **RNA stabilization**: Protecting transcripts from degradation.
*   **Translation**: Modulating the efficiency of protein synthesis by binding to mRNA 5' untranslated regions.

A smaller subset of PPR proteins are localized to the nucleus and can be involved in nuclear RNA processing or gene regulation. The "rna_processing" pathway assignment is highly consistent with the known functions of PPR proteins.
