### 1. FUNCTION: DNA-directed primase/polymerase

**KNOWN FACTS:**
*   **General Function:** DNA-directed primases are RNA polymerases that synthesize short RNA primers on a DNA template, which are then extended by DNA polymerases. DNA polymerases synthesize new DNA strands using existing DNA as a template. Together, primase and polymerase activities are fundamental for DNA replication and repair in all living organisms.
*   **Spinach Specificity:** The annotation "DNA-directed primase/polymerase" suggests a bifunctional enzyme or a complex containing both activities. In eukaryotes, DNA primase (a heterodimer of p49 and p58 subunits) is typically associated with DNA Polymerase alpha (Pol α) to form the Pol α-primase complex, which initiates DNA synthesis at replication origins and on the lagging strand.

These enzymes are essential for genome integrity and cell proliferation.
