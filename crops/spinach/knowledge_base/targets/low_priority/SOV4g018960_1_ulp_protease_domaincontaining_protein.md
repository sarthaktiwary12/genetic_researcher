### 1. FUNCTION: ULP_PROTEASE domain-containing protein

**KNOWN FACTS:**
*   ULP stands for Ubiquitin-Like-specific Protease. This family of enzymes is primarily known for its role in processing and deconjugating ubiquitin-like modifiers, most notably Small Ubiquitin-like Modifier (SUMO) proteins.
*   In eukaryotes, ULP proteases are often referred to as SUMO-specific proteases (SENPs) or isopeptidases. They perform two main functions:
    1.  **Maturation of SUMO precursors:** Cleaving the C-terminus of newly synthesized SUMO precursors to generate mature SUMO.
    2.  **De-SUMOylation:** Reversing SUMOylation by cleaving the isopeptide bond between SUMO and its target proteins. This removes SUMO from modified proteins, thereby regulating their activity, stability, localization, or interaction partners.

**INFERRED CONCLUSIONS (based on Arabidopsis homologs):**
*   Given the "protein_turnover" pathway assignment and the ULP_PROTEASE domain, SOV4g018960.1 is highly likely to encode a SUMO-specific protease (SENP).

*   The specific function of a SENP depends on its subcellular localization, substrate specificity, and expression pattern.
